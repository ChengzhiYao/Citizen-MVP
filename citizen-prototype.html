0 · Citizen already has a family safety layer — here's where it falls short real app screenshots · walk through one by one

Citizen already has the ingredients of a family safety product — trusted contacts, alert zones, location sharing, and nearby danger signals. But today they mostly operate as passive settings and notifications. The opportunity is to turn them into an active protection loop.

01 · Where the safety tools live (high level)

The safety layer exists — but it's hidden inside setup screens

Safety Network, Alert Zones, Friends and Location Sharing all live here as static profile settings — each one you set up manually, and then it just sits in the background.

Pain point: these tools are treated like profile settings, not active safety moments. You set them once, then rarely see how they help during a real walk, commute, or night out — they never come together into one active safety experience.
02 · Notifying the people who matter

It can alert loved ones about danger near you

"Nearby Activity — instant safety alerts when dangerous activity happens close to loved ones." It pings the people who care about you when something's up around you.

Pain point: it creates awareness, but not intervention. Loved ones get notified, but the product doesn't yet guide the user toward a safer next step.
The opportunity: turn alerts into a protection flow — check-in, reroute, share ETA, call a contact, or confirm "I'm safe."
03 · Registered offenders

It shows offenders near you or your loved ones

"See offender details and get alerted if an offender registers near you or your loved one's location."

Pain point: the information is high-emotion but low-action. It may raise awareness, but without guidance, users may not know what to do next.
The opportunity: not to make users stare at danger dots, but to help them adjust their routines — avoid certain blocks, keep an eye on a loved one's area, or set alerts around home, school, or work.

The core issue: Citizen currently sells access to more safety information. The stronger product opportunity is to offer an active protection layer — helping users and their loved ones know what to do next, not just what happened nearby. (That's Guardian — and it's what the live demo shows.)

1 · Walk With Me — one-tap live escort

Before

Safety Network, Friends and Location Sharing exist only as static profile settings showing "0" — passive, set-and-forget, rarely used in the moment.

  • No in-the-moment safety action
  • Network value invisible until an emergency
After

One tap from the map: share a live moving route + ETA with trusted people; auto-alert if you stop or arrive late; "I arrived safely" to end. Free.

  • Activates the network users already set up
  • A daily reason to open the app proactively

2 · Safe Route — incident-aware routing

Before

Citizen shows incidents on a map but does nothing with them for navigation. To avoid a dangerous block you have to eyeball the map, then plan a route yourself in a separate app.

  • Rich incident data goes unused for routing
  • No bridge between "what's happening" and "how I get there safely"
After

Enter a start + destination; Citizen scans active incidents along the way and suggests a detour (e.g. "Take Jackson Ave instead — +3 min, avoids the incident on 44th Dr"). It hands off to Google Maps by passing the detour as a waypoint, so Maps is nudged onto the safer path. CTA is "Open safer route in Google Maps" — never "guaranteed safe" — with an upfront note that Maps may re-route on live traffic.

  • Turns incident data into a real routing input
  • Honest handoff: waypoint nudge, not a false guarantee
  • Works with the app people already navigate in

3 · AI you can see — a calm, trustworthy summary

Before

Where AI touches ranking or copy it's unlabelled. Users can't tell what's machine-generated, what data it used, or whether it might be wrong. Feeds are raw and alarming.

  • No disclosure → erodes trust on a safety app
  • "Is this about me?" takes scrolling to answer
After

Every AI element carries an "AI-generated" badge with an expandable "How this was made" (what it did, sources, data used, a "can be wrong / call 911" caveat). A calm AI Incident Summary leads with a one-line recap + personal relevance. AI can be toggled off.

  • Full transparency, per the public-safety bar
  • Relevance answered in one glance
  • AI assists; verification stays human
